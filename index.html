General Queries

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents are usually needed for a basic title review? +
Typically, copies of 7/12 extracts, mutation entries (Ferfar), relevant Index II entries and earlier sale deeds / agreements are helpful. In layout or flat matters, layout plans and approvals may also be required. Exact requirements depend on the specific case.
Can every risk in a property transaction be ruled out through documents alone? +
No. Document review helps understand many aspects but may not reveal every possible issue. Certain aspects may require independent enquiries, revenue office inputs, survey work or other checks. This is generally discussed case by case.
Is this website giving legal advice for my situation? +
No. The information here is general in nature and only describes the type of work commonly handled. It is not tailored to your specific facts and is not legal advice. For any concrete guidance, you should share your documents and background and obtain a specific opinion.
